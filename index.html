Sparse coding models of natural images and sounds have been able to predict several response properties ofneurons in the visual and auditory systems. While the success of these models suggests that the structurethey capture is universal across domains to some degree, it is not yet clear which aspects of thisstructureare universal and which vary across sensory modalities. To address this, we fit complete and highlyovercomplete sparse coding models to natural images and spectrograms of speech and report on differencesin the statistics learned by these models.
